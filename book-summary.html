Themes Emerging From Chapter 1&2 Of “Born A Crime”


One of the issues emerging from Trevor Noah’s “Born a Crime”, book is religion. Trevor Noah sheds light on how religion played a key role in his
life, where I come from this has also always been one of the motors we lived with, from when I was little church was always a must, and every time
we had the chance of hearing about a revival service in a church within our neighborhood we were always front in line. Till today even in my adulthood
my friends who grew up together in the same area always sit and laugh about how our parents still always order us to attend church services. This
is something I have personally experienced and also heard from people Around me.
Another emerging issue that I can relate to from where I come from is tribalism, whereby you find that the way we’ve been raised to relate with
people within our tribe more comfortably than those of others. Sometimes it even reaches a point where some of us are not allowed to marry outside
our cultures and those who do undergo isolation from the community.
Although such cases are reducing with time some people still choose to have specific beliefs about other communities, especially with the rise of
many women taking up professions that were only believed to facilitate men.Those women end up being perceived as though they wouldn’t
possibly be able to do even half of the work men do in those professions and that their place is at home taking care of the children and doing house
chores .
