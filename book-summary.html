Themes Emerging From Chapter 1&2 Of Born A Crime


One of the themes emerging from Trevor Noah’s Born a Crime book is religion. He sheds light on how religion played a key role in his life. Since my life as a child this has also always been a rule at home , from when I was just a child church was always a must, and every time we had the chance of hearing of a revival service in a church within our neighborhood we were always front in line. Till this day even in my adulthood, my friends with similar experiences, always sit and laugh about how our parents still always order us to attend church services. This is something I have personally experienced and also heard from people Around me.

Another emerging theme is tribalism, whereby you find that the way we’ve been raised to relate with people within our tribe more comfortably than those of others. Sometimes it even reaches a point where some of us are not allowed to marry outside our cultures and those who do undergo isolation from the community.

Although such cases are reducing with time some people still choose to have specific beliefs about other communities, especially with the rise of many women taking up professions that were only believed to facilitate men.Those women end up being perceived as though they wouldn’t possibly be able to do even half of the work men do in those professions and that their place is at home taking care of the children and doing house chores.
